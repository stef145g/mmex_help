Stocks and Shares

Stock Portfolio or Managed Fund Accounts in MMEX

In order to use Stocks, we need to create an account of type: Investment

This is maintained in the Navigation Tree under the heading: Stocks

Managed Fund Account

A Managed Fund Account is where we only want to see the stocks that are held in a portfolio.

 Here we can create entries for the companies maintained by the fund and allocate the shares as a percentage of the total shares.

Example: We have a Stock Account with three Companies having a 33% of the total unit allocations. If the total units or Shares are 1000, each company would hold 333.33 units

Stock Portfolio Account

Initially, this is the same as a Managed Fund Account. The Stock Portfolio Account extends the Managed Fund Account concept by adding Share Accounts to maintain transactions for the specific Share Name. Here the number of shares are expressed as whole units. As a result we can record our initial share purchases, extended share purchases or sales as well as recording the dividends we recieve from the shares.

The stock broker name is used to define the Stock Investment name. The companies we by shares from become the share name, with an associated Share Account name the same as the Share Name.

Example:
Companies A, C and E maintain their accounts with Google Finance.
We create an account Google Finance and specify the type as Investment.
Using the Google Finance Account, we create New entries for:
Company A having 500 shares, Company C having 200 shares and company E having 300 shares.

 

 

 

Share Accounts in MMEX

As seen in the above screen, we can hide the Purchase Date, Unit Price and Price Date columns as these become obsolete.
We then create Share Accounts using the same name as the Share Name

Because a Shares account maintains the transactions for the company in our Stock account, we need to associate the share name to our share account.

For each Share Name and its associated Share Account we need to set the initial transaction for the account. This is done as follows:

  1. Use Add Trans from the Stock Portfolio view
  2. The first transaction needs to use the default values in the Stock Investment Details section
  3. Subsequent transactions need to provide the necessary details.
  4. The missing transaction details need to be provided.

 As for all transactions, we can also use the company name as a Payee, which we can create. It  is also advisable to create categories of Share: Purchase, Share: Dividend, Share: Sale and income: Share Dividend

 

Share Accounts

These accounts do not display on the Home page as these accounts are assistants to the Stock Account. Share accounts become part of the general accounts functionality section. Transfers to general accounts are handled the same as general accounts. Transfers to Stock accounts behave differently. These accounts can also have money being directly deposited and withdrawn as the case for Dividend  transactions. By accessing the Stock Account directly, the transactions for the account can be seen. The current visibility of Stock Accounts on the navigation page may change as further development occurs.

Adding Shares to our Share Accounts

The addition of Shares is done from the Stock Investment screen by selecting the company entry. This is done using the button: Add Trans

  1. The number of shares is populated from the created entry on the Stock Investment screen. Subsequent addition of shares are manually entered. These shares are then added to the share count on the Stock Investment screen.
  2. On the transaction side, any account name can be selected. Suggest that a share account be created with the same name as Note 2.
  3. For a share purchase, money obtained for Stocks would be a withdrawal from general accounts. This money is classified as a Transfer. The Transfer  checkbox allows us to treat this transaction as a withdrawal from the general accounts section. We can also set this to become a Deposit for the case when recording dividend payments, or a deposit transfer in the case of share sales.
Subsequent use of this screen allows us to add further transactions such as Dividend payments, More share purchases in the case when we have a Dividend Reinvestment Plan in operation. The DRP allows us to convert dividends to extra shares, without the use of a stock broker.

Use of Share Accounts
 
  1. Tthe use of a Share account is highly reccomended.
    Money can be transfered into  or out of  of these accounts without affecting the general accounts balance.
  2. Creating a new transaction from a Share account will connect directly to the general accounts.
  3. Money can be deposited or withdrawan from a Share account which will affect the balance, or it can be transfered into or out of the Share account.

Note: Future access to the shares accounts may be accessed via the Stocks Account in future.

Adding Dividends to our Shares.

Using the Add Trans dialog, we are able to add dividend payments to our individual share accounts

Note: The dialog will not display any share information when opened a second time on a share account.

  • Adding more shares, requires the addition of share information as well as the payee and category information.

  • Adding Dividends, share information is set to 0 and the type is set to Deposit.

  • The Transfer checkbox can be unticked for the transaction to become an Income on Deposit.

 

Examination of the individual Share Accounts will display the transactions for the shares and dividends.
